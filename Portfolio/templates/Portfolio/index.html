Parrot

Problem

In 2020, the CoVid-19 epidemic forced families, friends, and coworkers to social distance themselves and work remotely. People missed more than meetings, they missed life moments. Multiple video conferencing apps commanded the market including Zoom, Google, Facebook, and Discord. Unfortunately, these products still have major flaws in the fundamental user experience, accessibility, and data security.

Solution

Parrot is designed to put the user and those life moments first. With only one click from the home page or from an email invitation, the user instantly joins the video call in their web browser. For maximum accessibility, security, and stability, I designed and developed Parrot to have no need for mobile or desktop applications, no need for a login or data collection, and no need for on-site servers. CTFNation

Problem

In 2018, the field of cyber security was quickly gaining popularity but there were hardly any resources for beginners to learn the skills step by step in an entertaining way. A company called Hack The Box attempted to gamify the learning process with self guided real world exercises, however, the initial setup was complex and exercises lacked user assistance and community.

Solution

I designed and developed an open source web platform for hackers and cybersecurity enthusiasts to compete for glory in a variety of multiplayer games within a series of vulnerable virtual machines.

With an intuitive 3-step interface the user can easily select a game type, match with other players, and copy/paste the needed Linux command for instant setup and access. After 3 years of development, Hack The Box announced a Beta version for a similar concept and I released my project to the public. Captain Fatty’s Brewery

Problem

In 2017, I stumbled upon a small craft brewery inconveniently placed between busy loading docks and the local police station. They had been open for four years already and yet their web presence was near invisible. At the time, their website was missing the hours of operation, lacked cohesive branding across pages, and remained unresponsive to mobile platforms.

Solution

As a class project, I later designed and developed a fully functional conceptual website with a responsive mobile first design and robust SEO markup. The hours of operation were now displayed at the top on all pages along with a consistent brand personality and color scheme. I also created a simpler navigation architecture for the user to quickly find new events, beers, and merchandise.
